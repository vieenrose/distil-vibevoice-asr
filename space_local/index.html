How it works · accuracy

Audio is transcribed in 5-minute windows by a 0.9B model (Whisper-Medium encoder + Qwen3-0.6B decoder, fine-tuned for zh-TW meetings). Every segment gets an ECAPA-TDNN voice fingerprint, and one global clustering pass links speakers across windows — S01 at minute 3 is the same voice as S01 at minute 130. Everything (model, audio, results) stays in this tab.

Pipeline validated on real meetings vs pyannote references: DER 0.056 on a 30-min session, speaker consistency 0.912 on a 123-min session. Speed depends on your hardware (WebGPU is used when available): a 30 s clip is seconds; an hour of audio can take tens of minutes to hours of browser compute. The transcript streams while it works and you can stop anytime, keeping partial results. The same ONNX graphs power a sherpa-onnx C++ port for phones.

Example audio: 立法院 IVOD (Taiwan Legislative Yuan) open data, CC-BY-4.0, via openfun/tw-ly-ivod. Model: Apache-2.0 fine-tune of MOSS-Transcribe-Diarize. Machine-generated transcripts may contain errors.
